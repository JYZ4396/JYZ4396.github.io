Predicting High-Unusual Coastal Debris Events

I developed a binary classification project using coastal-cleanup records from 2015 to 2026. The target identifies cleanup events in the top 25% of unusual-debris share.

I compared Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting models using accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, ROC-AUC, and PR-AUC. I also conducted a no-year robustness check and constructed a cleaner 2023–2025 model after identifying strong temporal dependence in the full-period model.

Gradient Boosting produced the strongest predictive performance. The restricted model showed that geographic variables, including cleanup zone, longitude, and latitude, were among the most important predictors.
